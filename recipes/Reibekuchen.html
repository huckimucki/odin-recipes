Reibekuchen

Why you'll want to make Reibekuchen

There just isn't anything better than coming home from a walk on a rainy afternoon and warming yourself up with a couple of freshly baked Reibekuchen. The english translation potato pancake sounds like somethings children would cook, if they were allowed to mix anything together. So I'm sticking with Reibekuchen throughout this recipe.

The Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1.5kg potatoes
  • 3 onions
  • ~200g 405 wheat flour
  • 2 eggs
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Oil
  • Topping of your choice:
    • Molasses
    • Sour cream and chives
    • Apple sauce

Instructions

  1. Wash the potatoes and remove anything, that doesn't look edible. Like sprouts, green marks, ingrown soil etc.
  2. Coarsely grate potatoes, chop and add the onions to the grated potatoes.
  3. Add a lot of salt> (trust me, it's 1.5kg of potatoes. They need it) and a bit of pepper.
  4. Crack in the 2 pepper and mix everything together.
  5. Now add the flour bit by bit. I recommend starting with 4 tbs, mixing everything together. If you feel, that the mass needs more flour, you can add more right away or you fry the first portion and then see if the Reibekuchen need more flour.
  6. Heat your pan to medium-high and add 4-5 tbs oil.
  7. As soon as the oil starts shimmering in your frying pan, add a generous tbs of your potato-mixture to the pan (Caution! Hot oil!), making one portion. You'll want to have 3 - 4 portions at all times in your frying pan, almost swimming in oil. Flip the Reibekuchen after 3 - 4 minutes. Remember to add more oil, if they start to smoke too much. As soon as they are golden brown on both sides, put them down on a plate with a paper towel on it. Cover with another layer of paper towel and set aside.
  8. Repeat this process until done.
  9. Put a couple of perfectly cooked Reibekuchen on your plate and add them with the topping of your choice.
